UPSC Civil Services Exam Details

Eligibility

Core Eligibility Criteria

Candidates must be citizens of India, hold a degree from a recognized university, and be between 21-32 years of age, with relaxations for reserved categories.

Exam Pattern

Three-Stage Process

The exam consists of three stages: Preliminary (2 objective papers), Mains (9 descriptive papers), followed by a final Personality Test (Interview).

Syllabus

Preliminary Syllabus

Covers current events, history, geography, polity, economy, environment, and general science (GS Paper I), plus a qualifying aptitude test (CSAT).

Syllabus

Mains Syllabus

Includes nine extensive papers covering Essay, Indian language, English, and four General Studies papers, plus two papers on an optional subject.

Physical Standards

Physical Test (IPS/IRS)

Specific height, chest, and vision standards are mandatory for services like the Indian Police Service (IPS) and others requiring physical fitness.

Cut-off Marks

Previous Year Trends

Cut-offs are determined based on the number of vacancies and the difficulty of the paper. Analyzing past trends is crucial for setting a target score.

Key Dates

Important Schedule

The UPSC releases its annual calendar in advance. Typically, notification is in February, Prelims in June, and Mains around September.
